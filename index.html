PARALLAX

Parallax scrolling is a popular web design technique that creates a sense of depth and immersion by making background images move more slowly than foreground content as users scroll down a webpage. This effect leverages the perception of relative motion, which mimics how we perceive depth in the real world, adding a dynamic and engaging layer to web interfaces. The Concept of Parallax The term "parallax" originates from the Greek word "parallaxis," meaning "alteration." In the context of web design, it refers to the apparent shift in the position of an object when viewed from different angles or distances. This effect is commonly observed in real life when looking out of a moving vehicle: nearby objects pass by quickly, while distant objects appear to move more slowly. By replicating this phenomenon on a webpage, designers can create a three-dimensional experience within a two-dimensional medium. Technical Implementation Implementing parallax scrolling involves a combination of HTML and CSS. The basic idea is to manipulate the background and foreground elements' movement speed relative to the scrolling action.
